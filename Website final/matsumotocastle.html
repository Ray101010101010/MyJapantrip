MATSUMOTO CASTLE

the inner castle

Matsumoto Castle, also known as the “Crow Castle” for its striking black exterior, is one of Japan’s most iconic and well-preserved castles. Built in the 16th century, it features a unique wooden interior, stone foundations, and elegant defensive architecture. Surrounded by a picturesque moat and the backdrop of the Japanese Alps, the castle offers a glimpse into Japan’s feudal history and is a must-visit for history enthusiasts and photographers alike.

mypersonal experience

Known as the “Crow Castle” because of its striking black walls, it immediately stood out against the backdrop of the blue sky. Seeing it in person felt surreal, it’s one of the oldest surviving castles in Japan, and you can sense that history the moment you approach it. Walking across the moat and looking up at the towers, I was struck by how imposing the structure is. Inside, the castle still has its original wooden interiors and steep staircases, which made exploring quite difficult. Each floor had something to discover, from weapon displays to views of the city and mountains through the narrow windows once used for defence.

What I loved most was climbing all the way to the top floor. From there, the view of Matsumoto and the surrounding mountains was incredible it gave me a sense of how perfectly placed the castle is within its landscape. Matsumoto Castle wasn’t just a sightseeing stop for me it was an experience of Japan’s history, architecture, and natural beauty all in one. Standing there, surrounded by so much history, I understood why it’s considered one of the country’s most treasured landmarks.
