ONIOSHIDA

volcanic castle

Onioshida is an area nestled along the Kiso River in Nagano Prefecture, celebrated for its natural beauty and peaceful atmosphere. Surrounded by lush mountains and clear river waters, it’s a perfect spot for nature walks, photography, and experiencing rural Japan.

My personal experience

Exploring Onio Shida Castle was like stepping back in time. Wandering through the ruins gave me a real sense of history and imagination. As I walked along the old stone walls and paths, I tried to picture what life must have been like when the castle stood centuries ago.

It felt both peaceful and mysterious, as if the stones were still holding onto the stories of the past. Unlike some of the more restored castles in Japan, Onio Shida feels authentic, giving you space to quietly explore. Even though the structures are mostly remnants, I loved the experience of just being there, surrounded by the ruins and the view. It was one of those places where you can feel Japan’s deep cultural history, tucked away in a setting that’s beautiful.
