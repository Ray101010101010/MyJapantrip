KITAYAMA

Luscious forests

Kitayama, a charming district in Kyoto, is known for its historic streets that capture the essence of old Japan. The area offers peaceful walks through lush greenery, with iconic bamboo forests. Visitors can also explore nearby temples and gardens, where the harmony of nature and architecture reflects Japan’s cultural beauty.

My personal experience

Visiting Kitayama felt like stepping into a calmer section of Japan. Surrounded by mountains and deep forests, the area has this quiet charm that makes you want to pause and take everything in. What stood out to me most was how peaceful it was no crowds, just the sound of the steady flows of small streams rushing through the landscape. I wandered through the village paths and took in the traditional wooden houses. There was a sense of authenticity here that I found refreshing compared to the busier tourist spots.

The views from higher up in Kitayama were breathtaking. The kind of scenery that makes you stop and really appreciate the beauty of rural Japan. For me, Kitayama wasn’t just a place to explore, but a place to feel a reminder of how special rural Japan can be.
