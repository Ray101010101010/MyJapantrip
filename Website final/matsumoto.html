MATSUMOTO

line between grass and stone

Matsumoto, located in Nagano Prefecture, is a city rich in history and culture, best known for its stunning Matsumoto Castle, one of Japan’s most beautiful original castles. Surrounded by the Japanese Alps, the city offers a mix of scenic landscapes, traditional streets, and vibrant local markets. Matsumoto is also a gateway to outdoor adventures, art museums, and regional cuisine, making it a perfect destination to experience both Japan’s heritage and natural beauty.

my personal experience

Visiting Matsumoto turned out to be one of the best parts of my trip. While the castle is the city’s star attraction, the town itself has a warm and welcoming feel that made me enjoy just wandering around. The streets are lined with a mix of old wooden shops and modern cafes, giving Matsumoto a unique feel of history and everyday life.

As i walked it felt like walking through history while still being part of a lively, modern city. The highlight was how close Matsumoto is to nature. The backdrop of the Japanese Alps is breathtaking, and just stepping outside the main streets you can already feel the fresh mountain air. It’s the kind of city that feels both cultural and natural at the same time. For me, Matsumoto wasn’t just about sightseeing, it was about feeling. It had that slower, relaxed pace where you can enjoy good food and beautiful scenery all at once.
